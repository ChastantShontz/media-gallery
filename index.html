The first major conflict between the Jedi and Sith was the Great Hyperspace War. For a century before this, the Sith Empire lived in isolation under the stable rule of Marka Ragnos. However, following Ragnos's death, a power struggle erupted between the rival Sith Lords Naga Sadow and Ludo Kressh. The discovery of the Sith homeworld of Korriban by the hyperspace scouts Gav and Jori Daragon brought the Empire into contact with the Galactic Republic, and an invasion led by Sadow commenced. Despite his usage of battle meditation to bolster his forces, Sadow was ultimately defeated by Empress Teta. His flagship crash landed on Yavin 4, where he lived out the remainder of his life in exile.

After almost thirty years of war between the Galactic Republic and the Sith Empire, the conflict was brough to an end with the Sacking of Coruscant. Mentally influenced by the imprisoned Jedi Revan, the Sith Emperor decided to vie for peace. While the Republic was distracted with the negotiations on Alderaan, the Sith Lord Darth Malgus led an attack on the Jedi Temple on Coruscant, decimating it. Holding Coruscant hostage, the Empire was then able to force the Republic into signing the unfavorable Treaty of Coruscant. Under these terms, half of the galaxy was handed over to the Empire, ushering in a new era of Cold War that lasted for over a decade.

The Sith Lord Darth Bane orchestrated the destruction of the flawed Brotherhood of Darkness using the thought bomb. This was a complex dark side ritual that instantly vaporized all Force-sensitive beings within its vacinity, including the ones who created it. After one thousand years of constant warfare between the Jedi and Sith, culminating in a two-year-long campaign on Ruusan, both sides were on the brink of destruction. Bane, who had grown disillusioned with the Brotherhood's philosophy of all Sith being equals, manipulated its leader Skere Kaan into using the thought bomb against the Army of Light. Afterward, as the sole surviving Sith, Bane established the Rule of Two, which dictated that there could be no more than two Sith at a time: a master and an apprentice.

The most descructive conflict in galactic history was the Yuuzhan Vong invasion. The Yuuzhan Vong were alien refugees from another galaxy, which was largely destroyed in terrible civil war. For fifteen thousand years, they wanted between galaxies looking for a new one to call home. When the Vong finally settled on a galaxy to invade, the once hostile New Republic and Imperial Remnant were forced into an uneasy alliance to combat them. Victory was only achieved with help from the sentient extra-galactic planet Zonama Sekot, the seed of the Vong's own homeworld Yuuzhan'tar. Supreme Overlord Shimrra Jamaane was killed by Luke Skywalker, and the Vong were exiled from the galaxy onboard Sekot.

Haazen

A failed Jedi who attempted to rule over both the Jedi and Sith by enhancing his damaged body with their artifacts.

Unlike his best friend, the renowned Jedi Barrison Draay, Haazen possessed very little talent with the Force. Despite failing his Jedi training as a result of this, he continued serving Barrison as a steward.

During the Great Sith War, Haazen was approached by a Sith minion named Dossa, who convinced him to join her cause by stoking his jealousy toward Barrison. Haazen led Barrison into a trap set by Dossa, although she betrayed him and detonated the charges before he could get clear. Barely alive, Haazen's damaged body was rebuilt with cybernetic parts. He then killed Dossa, vowing to someday surpass both the Jedi and Sith.

After the war, Barrison's wife Krynda Draay founded the Jedi Covenant, an extremist sect of the Jedi Order dedicated to preventing the return of the Sith. Haazen continued to serve Krynda, who was unaware of the role he played in Barrison's death. This position in the Covenant allowed him to acquire many Jedi and Sith artifacts, building them into his body to enhance himself.

At the height of the Mandalorian Wars, Haazen launched a Covenant coup against the Jedi Order by claiming that the Jedi High Council had been influenced by the Sith. He also took control of the Republic fleet and used it to fire on Coruscant, while protected from all physical harm by the Gauntlet of Kressh the Younger. Haazen planned to build parallel armies of Jedi and Sith under his command, the Jedi led by the failed Padawan Zayne Carrick and the Sith led by Barrison and Krynda's son Lucien Draay. However, Carrick managed to trick Haazen into relinquishing the Gauntlet of Kressh the Younger, allowing him to be killed in the orbital bombardment.

Darth Traya

The founder of the Sith Triumvirate who later trained Meetra Surik to destroy them after being betrayed by Darth Nihilus and Darth Sion.

Once an esteemed Jedi Master, Kreia's reputation was ruined when her most famous Padawan, Revan, disobeyed the Jedi High Council to aid the Galactic Republic in the Mandalorian Wars. Exiled from the Jedi Order as a result of this, she discovered the Trayus Academy on Malachor V, which had been used by Revan to train Sith Apprentices during his time as the Sith Lord Darth Revan.

This dark side nexus corrupted Kreia to the dark side and she became Darth Traya, the Lord of Betrayal. Traya took on a pair of apprentices, the Lord of Hunger Darth Nihilus and the Lord of Pain Darth Sion. Together, they formed the Sith Triumvirate.

However, Nihilus and Sion eventually betrayed Traya, stripping her of her Force-sensitivity and casting her out. They then went on to almost completely wipe out the Jedi with the First Jedi Purge. Having been betrayed by both the Jedi and Sith, Traya now sought to free the galaxy of the Force's influence and took back her old name of Kreia.

She found a new apprentice in the former Jedi Meetra Surik, who had also been exiled for her participation in the Mandalorian Wars and cut herself off from the Force during its traumatic final battle. Kreia trained Surik to regain this connection and helped her find the Jedi Enclave on Dantooine. Revealing her true identity, she killed all the Jedi Masters there.

Kreia became Darth Traya again, breaking Sion's will and enslaving him. She then manipulated Surik into killing both Nihilus and Sion. As Surik's final test, she killed Traya as well at the Trayus Academy.

Revan

A Jedi who was brainwashed into becoming a Sith Lord before turning back to the light side and attempting to kill the Sith Emperor.

The Jedi who would later be known as Revan trained under many Jedi Masters, predominantly Kreia. When the Mandalorians invaded the Galactic Republic, the Jedi High Council forbid the Jedi from interfering in the war. However, Revan and his friend Alek Squinquargesimus disobeyed the Council and led a splinter group of like-minded Jedi called the Revanchists in defense of the Republic.

On Cathar, they came across the aftermath of a massacre where the Mabdalorians had wiped out almost the entire Cathar species by herding them into the ocean. One Mandalorian had objected to this, only to be executed along with them. Donning her mask, this Jedi declared himself Revan and vowed to destroy the Mandalorians.

In a final battle above Malachor V, Revan killed their leader, Mandalore the Ultimate. With his dying breath, Mandalore revealed that he had been manipulated into invading the Republic by the Sith.

Revan and Alek embarked on a search for this mysterious Sith in the Unknown Regions. They eventually discovered the resurgent Sith Empire on Dromund Kaas and infiltrated it in an attempt to assassinate the Sith Emperor. However, he easily defeated them and twisted them to the dark side, renaming them Darth Revan and Darth Malak.

The Emperor sent Revan and Malak to find the Star Forge, an ancient Rakatan superweapon capable of producing infinite weapons of war. They succeeded, but betrayed the Emperor and decided to invade the Republic on their own.

Revan was later betrayed in turn by Malak when a strike team of Jedi led by Bastila Shan attacked him. Malak fired on Revan's flagship, presumably killing him. In truth, Revan survived and was healed by Bastila, who wiped his memory and gave him a new identity as a Republic soldier.

Revan gradually rediscovered his Force-sensitivity and trained as a Jedi again under Bastila. He retraced his steps to the Star Forge and destroyed it, as well as killing Malak. Afterward, he and Bastila were married.

Still, Revan was haunted by nightmares of the Sith on Dromund Kaas. With help from the Mandalorian Canderous Ordo, he left to find it again, leaving Bastila pregnant with their son Vaner Shan. However, he was captured again by the Sith Lords Darth Nyriss and Scourge, who held him prisoner for years.

Revan was later freed when his Jedi friend Meetra Surik came looking for him. Scourge also joined Revan and Surik in their quest to kill the Emperor after learning that he planned to eradicate all life in the universe with a dark side ritual.

During their confrontation with the Emperor, Scourge had a vision of another Jedi called the Hero of Tython defeating the Emperor and realized that Revan was not powerful enough. He betrayed them, killing Surik and capturing Revan, so he could bide his time until the Hero appeared.

The Emperor imprisoned Revan in statis for over three hundred years, probing his mind in an attempt to learn the Republic's secrets. Revan was able to use this connection to manipulate the Emperor into vying for peace with the Treaty of Coruscant.

After eventually being freed by the Jedi, Revan sought to destroy the Emperor with an army of droids that he created using another Rakatan superweapon called the Foundry. He was defeated by a Sith strike team led by Darth Malgus and vanished in a flash of light.

Rather than dying, though, Revan split into light and dark halves. While his light half existed as a spirit, ready to move on, his dark half clung to the physical world. This dark Revan assumed control of the Order of Revan, a cult worshipping him that had infiltrated both the Republic and the Empire.

Revan found the Emperor, who had since been defeated by the Hero, hiding as a spirit on Yavin 4. He planned to resurrect the Emperor so he could kill him permanently, although the Jedi and Sith formed an allience to stop him. After being defeated by them, dark Revan merged with the spirit that was light Revan, finally becoming one with the Force.

Revan's spirit later manifested in the mind of his descendent, Jedi Grand Master Satele Shan, to aid her in the final battle against the Emperor. Along with the spirits of everyone the Emperor had ever hurt, including Surik and Scourge, he was able to vanquish the Emperor once and for all.

Meetra Surik

An exiled Jedi who cut herself off from the Force before being trained by Kreia to destroy the Sith Triumvirate.

Despite the Jedi High Council resolving to stay out of the Mandalorian Wars, Meetra Surik was one of the Revanchists who disobeyed them and joined the fight. During the final battle above Malachor V, she activated a superweapon called the Mass Shadow generator that destroyed all ships on both sides by pulling them into the planet's surface.

Sensing all the deaths here was so traumatic for Surik that she cut herself off from the Force. Afterward, she was cast out of the Jedi Order for her participation in the Mandalorian Wars and became known as the Jedi Exile.

It was because of this that Surik was able to survive the First Jedi Purge enacted by the Sith Triumvirate. The Jedi Atris orchestrated her return to Republic space in order to draw out the Sith. Surik was joined in her quest by a mysterious individual named Kreia, who trained her to regain her connection to the Force.

Surik and Kreia sought help from the Jedi Enclave on Dantooine, only to be turned away because of Surik's banishment. Kreia then revealed her true identity as Darth Traya, founder of the Triumvirate, and kill all the Jedi Masters there.

Surik went on to kill another member of the Triumvirate, Darth Nihilus, with help from his former apprentice Visas Marr. She then confronted Traya and its last member, Darth Sion, at the Trayus Academy on Malachor V and killed them both.

Afterward, she left Republic space in search of the Revanchists's former leader Revan, who had vanished years ago. Surik retraced Revan's steps and discovered the resurgent Sith Empire on Dromund Kaas, where Revan was being held prisoner by the Sith Lord Scourge.

Knowing that the Sith Emperor planned to wipe out all life in the universe with a dark side ritual, Scourge helped Surik free Revan. However, while confronting the Emperor, Scourge had a vision of another Jedi named the Hero of Tython defeating the Emperor and realized that they couldn't win. He betrayed and killed Surik, as well as capturing Revan, so he could bide his time until the Hero appeared.

While Revan was imprisoned for over three hundred years, Surik's spirit continued to give him strength to resist the Emperor's mental probing. She also manifested in the mind of Jedi Grand Master Satele Shan to aid her in the final battle against the Emperor. The combined spirits of everyone the Emperor had ever hurt, including Revan and Scourge, were able to vanquish him once and for all.

Darth Bane

A Sith Lord in the Brotherhood of Darkness who orchestrated its destruction so that he could establish the Rule of Two.

As a result of his mother dying in childbirth, Dessel was relentlessly abused by his father Hurst for his entire childhood. One day, after nearly being beaten to death, Des used his latent Force-sensitivity to kill Hurst by giving him a heart attack.

Inheriting all of Hurst's debt, Des was forced to work in the cortosis mines on Apatros. He fled Apatros after accidentally killing a Republic officer in a brawl and joined the military of the Brotherhood of Darkness.

Des's strength in the Force was discovered by the Sith Lord Kopecz, who brought him to the Sith Academy on Korriban. There, he renamed himself Bane after Hurst's nickname for him. Despite being a very promising student initially, the realization that he had been responsible for Hurst's death caused Bane to temporarily cut himself off from the Force.

It was with help from the fellow Sith Apprentice Githany that Bane was able to regain his connection to the Force. Eventually, he was powerful enouogh to defeat the Academy's top student, Sirak. Throughout their training, Bane and Githany also started to develop feelings for each other.

Bane soon came to be disillusioned with the Brotherhood and its philosophy that all Sith were equal. This led him to leave and proclaim himself Darth Bane, a title which had been discarded centuries ago. Inspired by the holocron of the ancient Sith Lord Darth Revan, he sought to replace the Brotherhood with only two Sith at a time: a master and an apprentice.

After surviving several assassination attempts from the Brotherhood, Bane traveled to Ruusan, where it was in the middle of waging a two-year-long campaign against the Army of Light. He tricked its leader, Skere Kaan, into using a dark side ritual called the thought bomb that vaporized all Force-sensitive beings within its vacinity, wiping out the Brotherhood.

As he fled from the thought bomb, Bane came across an orphan girl named Rain who had just unintentionally used the Force to kill a pair of Jedi. He took her on as his apprentice, dubbing her Darth Zannah.

The Rule of Two was almost exposed a decade later when Zannah was reunited with her cousin the former Jedi Darovit. However, she was able to trick the other Jedi into killing Darovit under the belief that he was the last surviving Sith.

Bane eventually came to believe that Zannah was an unworthy apprentice due to her reluctance to challenge him for the mantle of Sith Master. He temporarily took on another one, a Force-sensitive assassin known as the Huntress. However, in a final duel on Ambria, Zannah destroyed Bane and took the Huntress on as her own apprentice.

Quinlan Vos

A Jedi during the Clone Wars who nearly turned to the dark side while infiltrating the Confederacy of Independent Systems.

Found to be Force-sensitive at a young age, Quinlan Vos was initially trained on his homeworld of Kiffu by the Jedi Tholme. After his great-aunt Tinte Vos sacrificed his parents, Quian and Pethros Vos, to Anzati vampires, Tholme brought Quinlan to the Jedi Temple on Coruscant.

On one mission, Vos saved a Force-sensitive girl named Aayla Secura from a wampa, before bring her back to the Jedi Order. Secura later became his Padawan once Vos had achieved the rank of Jedi Knight.

Vos and Secura were used by the corrupt Senator Chom Frey Kaa as a test subject for his drug glitteryll, which wiped both of their memories. With help from the smuggler Vilmarh Grahrk, Vos was able to uncover this conspiracy and bring Kaa to justice. He then returned to the Jedi to be retrained.

Secura, on the other hand, stumbled upon the ancient Dark Jedi Volfe Karkko, who had been imprisoned in stasis for a millennia. She briefly became his apprentice, although Karkko was killed by Vos.

During the Clone Wars, Vos went undercover in the Confederacy of Independent Systems as one of Dooku's Dark Acolytes. This deception carried on for so long that he nearly succumbed to the dark side for real, and even his closest allies were unaware of his true allegiance. He finally renounced the dark side for good during the Siege of Saleucami, destroying a cloned army of Morgukai assassins that the Confederacy was attempting to grow.

Vos was stationed on Kashyyyk when Order 66 was given, although he was able to survive with help from Grahrk. He then went into hiding with Tholme, who had also survived, his secret lover Khaleen Hentz, and their newborn son Korto Vos.

Dartht Krayt

A former Tusken Raider and Jedi named A'Sharad Hett who survived Order 66 only to turn to the dark side and found the One Sith.

Just like his father, the former Jedi Sharad Hett, A'Sharad Hett was a Human raised in the culture of the Tusken Raiders. He never knew his mother, another Human named K'Sheek, as she mysteriously vanished into a sandstorm shortly after his birth.

When A'Sharad was fifteen, Jabba the Hutt orchestrated a war between the Humans and Tuskens on Tatooine in order to increase demand for old weapons. Sharad was killed in this conflict by the Jedi-turned-bounty hunter Aurra Sing. Honoring his dying wish, the Jedi Ki-Adi-Mundi took A'Sharad back to the Jedi Order to become his Padawan.

Hett briefly considered leaving the Jedi after giving into the dark side during a rematch with Sing. Instead, he continued his training under An'ya Kuro, who specialized in "difficult" students. Hett later took on a Padawan of his own named Bhat Jul.

During the Clone Wars, Hett frequently fought alongside Anakin Skywalker. At one point, they had been good friends, but this friendship turned to hatred after Anakin's mother Shmi Skywalker was murdered by Tuskens.

Hett managed to survive Order 66 and returned to Tatooine, where he led the Tuskens to war against the Humans. His reign of terror here ended when he was defeated by Obi-Wan Kenobi. Exiled from Tatooine, he became a bounty hunter.

One of Hett's quaries led him to the Sith homeworld of Korriban. There, he studied the dark side from the holocron of the ancient Sith Lord XoXaan. He intended to use these skills to destroy Emperor Palpatine and Darth Vader, although by the time he left Korriban, they had already been defeated Luke Skywalker.

Fleeing into the Unknown Regions, Hett was captured by the extra-galactic Yuuzhan Vong. It was here that he met the Dark Jedi Vergere, who completed his conversion to the Sith. However, Vergere abandoned him when he expressed his belief that the Sith should seek strength in numbers instead of the Rule of Two.

After escaping the Vong, Hett adopted the name Darth Krayt and founded the One Sith on Korriban. For over a century, they remained in hiding and covertly manipulated galactic events. Krayt was forced to reveal himself on one occasion, forming an alliance with Luke to defeat the dark side entity Abeloth.

When Luke's grandson Kol Skywalker conceived a plan to use Vong technology to repair the damage inflicted by the Yuuzhan Vong War, the One Sith sabotaged this, orchestrating a war between the Galactic Alliance and the Fel Empire. They then revealed themselves and entered the conflict on the side of the Empire. Once the Jedi and the Alliance had been defeated, Krayt betrayed and usurped Emperor Roan Fel.

However, Krayt was dying from Yorik-Kul that the Vong had implanted in him. The only person capable of healing him was Kol's son Cade Skywalker due to his unique dark transfer ability. Krayt captured Cade and attempted to turn him to the dark side, but Cade was able to resist.

Krayt finally did learn to heal himself from the spirit of the ancient Sith Lord Karness Muur. Immediately after, he was betrayed and murdered by his right-hand man, Darth Wyyrlok. Krayt was able to use his newfound abilities to heal his body and resurrect himself, though.

With an army of new Sith troopers, Krayt killed Wyyrlok and reclaimed control of the One Sith. He planned to become immortal by transfering his essense into Cade's body, but was killed by Cade before he could do this. To prevent Krayt from healing himself again, Cade vaporized his body in Coruscant's sun.
